    Team Bright Pigs Place 2nd in Michigan Governor's High School Cyber Challenge

    Team Bright Pigs of the SITS group recently took home the 2nd place trophy during the 2019 Michigan Governor's High School Cyber Challenge. The challenge tests student's knowlege in cybersecurity and related topics. Teams of up to three high school students compete in a virtual Capture-the-Flag activity designed to test their skills and abilities. For more information, visit the official website.

    November 9th, 2021

    Canton High School Cyber Teams Sweep the Floors of Competition

    First, Second, and Third Place finishes for the Governor's High School Cyber Challenge go to Canton High School! In order for these students to accomplish such a tremendous feat, they had to beat over 200 different schools aroundt the state of Michigan. The importance of hardening computer systems for protecting against cyber threats is increasing every day and these students at Canton High School demonstrated they can fulfill that need. They are up to the challenge of constantly innovating and adapting to new technologies and challenges in the cyber space.

    First Place Team: Crickets from Canton High School

    From Left to Right: Eric D'Urso, David Reeves, and Jacob Eason

    Second Place Team: Breakfast Vedge from Canton High School

    From Left to Right: Alex Liao, and Jayden Sarveshkumar

    Third Place Team: Teamy McTeamer from Canton High School

    From Left to Right: Pranav Merugu, Fritz, Geib, Robert Everitt

    Computer Science teacher at PCEP - Scott Obsniuk - attributes all of this great work to the effort his students put into class.

    According to Mr. Obsniuk: "Honestly, all I do is sign them up, give them the rules and tell them what to expect and they go from there. It's so early on in the school year, we don't have a lot of time to cover a whole lot of content or practice for the competition, so it's really a compilation of what they've learned throughout different computer science courses."

    Mr. Obsniuk gives his students full credit for their great performance in the Michigan Cyber Security Governors Challenge

    When asked about some of the best tips for winning these challenges, these group members had very unique opinions:

    According to David Reeves: "You have to get into the hackers' minds, know what they're trying to do before you can prevent hacking. Every feature of a computer can be abused, so you have to be on the look out for suspicious-looking codes or red flags."

    Eric D'Urso also adds, "The most important thing people need to remember is to not put themselves in compromising positions."
